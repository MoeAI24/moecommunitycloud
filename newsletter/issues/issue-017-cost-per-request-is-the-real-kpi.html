Cost Per Request Is the Real KPI

2026-01-24 • Operations • MBCC

Monthly cloud cost is a lagging metric. Cost per request tells you whether your growth is profitable or painful.

Become a Partner → Use Free Tools → Done-for-you →

What this issue covers

  • Why cost per request is better than total cloud spend
  • What usually drives it up (egress, retries, overbuild, chatty services)
  • A simple plan to push it down without breaking reliability
Takeaway: If you can’t measure cost per request, you can’t scale with confidence.

Why cost per request wins

Total spend doesn’t explain efficiency. Cost per request does.

When traffic doubles, your cost per request should stay flat or improve — not explode.

What drives it up

Egress fees, retries, cold starts, over-provisioned compute, and overly chatty service calls.

Most teams pay twice: once in cloud bills, and again in engineer time.

How to push it down safely

Cache hot paths, reduce payload sizes, and kill unnecessary cross-service calls.

Set budgets and alerts by endpoint or workload, not just by account.

Quick action plan

  1. Run: Cost Reality Checker + Uptime Risk Score.
  2. Create a one-page proof pack: what changed, who approved it, what guardrails exist.
  3. Repeat weekly: measure → fix top bottleneck → document → repeat.
