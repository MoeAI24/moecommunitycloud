AI Is Getting Cheaper — Control Is Getting More Expensive

Issue • 002 • Ai • Cheaper • Control 2025-12-17
Join the Runway → Back to Newsletter

Most people are watching the price of intelligence drop. Enterprises are watching something else: the price of **control**.

When generation becomes cheap, the real premium shifts to everything that keeps a system safe, explainable, and operable in production. The model is no longer the product — the operating layer is.

Why “cheap AI” creates expensive risk

  • Cheap output increases volume. Volume increases exposure.
  • Exposure multiplies failure modes: drift, hallucinations, inconsistent behavior, and uncontrolled actions.
  • The cost shows up later as incident response, customer impact, and reputational damage.

The new premium is ownership and auditability

Enterprises pay for:

  • **Governance**: who can do what, when, and why.
  • **Evidence**: logs, evaluation results, approvals, and change history.
  • **Operations**: monitoring, escalation paths, and recovery procedures.
  • **Boundaries**: kill switches, human review gates, and permissioned tool access.

What to do right now

Treat “control” as a first-class deliverable. If you can’t explain an agent’s behavior, you can’t scale it. If you can’t prove it, you can’t sell it.

Build the system so the intelligence is replaceable — and the control layer is durable.
