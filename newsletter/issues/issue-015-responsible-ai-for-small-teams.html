Responsible AI for Small Teams

2026-01-24 • Responsible AI • MBCC

Responsible AI doesn’t require a giant committee. It requires thresholds, disclosures, and a habit of measuring what matters.

Consulting Help → Use Free Tools → Done-for-you →

What this issue covers

  • A simple responsibility checklist small teams can actually follow
  • Disclosures that reduce risk without scaring users
  • The minimum evaluation loop for safe iteration
Takeaway: Responsibility is a system: thresholds, monitoring, and fast correction.

Start with thresholds

Decide what the system is allowed to do and what must be reviewed.

High-risk actions should require human approval.

Disclose clearly

Tell users when AI is involved and what it can’t do.

Clear disclosures reduce support load and reputational risk.

Measure and correct

Track failure rates and user-reported issues.

Create a small weekly review loop to fix the top issue.

Quick action plan

  1. Run: Cost Reality Checker + Uptime Risk Score.
  2. Create a one-page proof pack: what changed, who approved it, what guardrails exist.
  3. Repeat weekly: measure → fix top bottleneck → document → repeat.
